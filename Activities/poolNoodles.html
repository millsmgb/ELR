  • How many pool noodle pieces are on each one?

Did you Know?

Crossing the midline

Crossing the body midline is the ability to reach across the middle of the body with the arms or legs to reach the opposite side.

An example of this is touching the opposite elbow or knee.

Being able to cross the body midline means the two hemispheres of the brain are communicating normally to synchronise learning and movement.

It encourages strengthening of the dominant hand through repeated use. When a child does not cross the midline, they use the closest hand to complete the task rather than switching to the dominant hand.
