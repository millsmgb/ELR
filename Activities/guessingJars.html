Extend it

To look at quantity and introduce the idea of standardised units, you can use jars of the same size to compare how the size of an object effects how many can be placed in a jar. You can also use jars of different sizes and fill it with different quantities of the same object.

Did you Know?

We are born mathematical and have an intuitive sense of cardinality. This is the understanding that groups of things have a number associated with them. This means that newborns can recognise when the number of items they’re looking at stays the same or changes. In other words, if you continually show them groups of two items, they will start to lose interest until this is changed to say three items. Try it with a baby and watch how they respond!

Tips

Unless you have large items to fill it with, don’t use jars that are too big. Alternatively, if you do want to use large items, you can also try different sized containers e.g. boxes, tubs, laundry baskets.
