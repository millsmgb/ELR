Did you Know?

The science behind the activity

Salt sprinkled on ice causes it to melt. Water usually freezes at 0 degrees Celsius, however the salt lowers the freezing point below zero. In order for the ice to melt, it has to absorb heat. The heat that is absorbs comes from the water near the ice cube. Some of this water becomes so cold that it refreezes and the string becomes trapped, allowing you to lif the ice cube out of the water.

Tips

  • Avoid giving the children the official answer

  • Encourage them to hypothesise (come up with a probable explanation) for what they observe
  • If you don't know, write down the question to research together
