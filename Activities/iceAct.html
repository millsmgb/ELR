This activity combines the things little ones love: ice, water, and a touch of wonder!

Materials

  • Ice cubes
  • Water
  • Salt
  • String
  • Pen
  • Small bowl

Step 1

Cut the string so that it is longer than twice the width of the bowl. Half-fill the bowl with water. Add an ice cube to the water.

Step 2

Ask your child to try and pick up the ice cube using the string. Spoiler Alert! They won't be able to pick it up.

Step 3

Lay the string across the top of the ice. Now sprinkle some salt on top. Wait for 60 seconds. Talk about what might happen next.

Step 4

Ask your child to grab both sides of the string and slowly lift. What happens?

Step 5

Talk about what might have made the ice stick to the string.

Extend it

  • Use different sized ice cubes

Did you Know?

The science behind the activity

Salt sprinkled on ice causes it to melt. Water usually freezes at 0 degrees Celsius, however the salt lowers the freezing point below zero. In order for the ice to melt, it has to absorb heat. The heat that is absorbs comes from the water near the ice cube. Some of this water becomes so cold that it refreezes and the string becomes trapped, allowing you to lif the ice cube out of the water.

Tips

  • Avoid giving the children the official answer
  • Prompt them to observe what happens to the ice when it comes into contact with the salt, what happens to the string whn it comes into contact with the ice and salt.
  • Encourage them to hypothesise (come up with a probable explanation) for what they observe
  • If you don't know, write down the question to research together
