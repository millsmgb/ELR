Cover Card

My little one just loved everything about this activity - the water play, the sound making and the colour changing!

Materials

  • Clean glass jars
  • Metal and plastic cutlery
  • Water
  • Food colouring (optional)

Step 1

Ask your child to tap each piece of cutlery in turn against the glass jars. Which material made the clearest sound, plastic or metal?

Step 2

Ask what might happen to the sound if we poured water into the jars. Let your child pour water into the jars, leaving one empty.

Step 3

Test the sound again with the cutlery of choice. Do the jars with water sound any different to the ones that don't?

Step 4

At this point, my little one added colouring using food colouring because the effect is just beautiful.

Did you Know?

Sound Waves

Did you know that sound travels through air at 340m per second? Sound can also travel through water and in fact moves four times faster through water than air! Sound can travel such long distances that whales can hear each other hundreds of kilometres apart.
