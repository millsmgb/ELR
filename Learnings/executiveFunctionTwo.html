Executive Function

Why is it so important?

Together, executive function skills allow your child to plan, reason,pay attention and multi-task

Your child needs to be able to do these activities to succeed in school, and in life

Real life applications

These skills are important because they help develop other abilities

For example, they can help your child to understand how other people might be feeling or thinking, or to learn a new skill like math or reading.

It doesn't work in isolation

Children with good executive functions have stronger emotional, social and moral skills and tend to be healthier later in life.

When does it develop?

Executive functions take many years to fully develop

However, there are two times in life when they develop quickly: during the preschool years, and at the the beginning of the teenage years.

Why hasn't my chld mastered these skills yet?

For children younger than four, executive functioning skills are underdeveloped

As a result, most young children find it hard to do things like resist temptations, plan ahead, concentrate, and control their emotions.
