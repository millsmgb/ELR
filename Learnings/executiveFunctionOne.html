Executive Function

Executive functioning doesn’t sound like something you’d connect with young children, yet it’s something we’re all born with the potential to develop from a young age.

There are three main processes that make executive functioning possible:

  • Working memory
  • Self-regulation
  • Mental flexibility

Let's look at each of these in turn

Working memory lets us store and work on information that we've recently come across. This ability helps children pay attention, act less impulsively and stay focused on their work.

Inhibitory Control

Inhibitory control is the skill that lets us organise our goals by letting us control our impulses and filter our thoughts. This skill is necessary to perform cognitive tasks such as relate topics to one another, mental calculation, and decide what needs to get done in order of priority.
